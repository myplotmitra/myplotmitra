Home Loan Guide for Buying Property in Anantapur

Most property purchases in Anantapur district are financed at least partly through a home loan. This guide covers the practical basics: what lenders check, what documents you'll need, and — importantly — the costs a home loan usually does not cover.

What lenders typically check

  • Income and repayment capacity (salary slips, IT returns, or business income proof for self-employed applicants)
  • Credit history and score
  • Property documents — including DTCP approval status for plots, since most banks are far more comfortable lending against approved layouts
  • Clear, marketable title with no pending disputes

Typical documents you'll need

  • Identity and address proof (Aadhaar, PAN)
  • Income proof (salary slips / IT returns / bank statements)
  • Property documents (sale deed, layout approval, encumbrance certificate)
  • Passport-size photographs

The cost your loan usually doesn't cover

This is the part many first-time buyers overlook: stamp duty, registration fee, and transfer duty — together roughly 7.5% of the property value in Andhra Pradesh — are generally paid separately, out of your own funds, not included in the sanctioned loan amount. See our full stamp duty and registration guide for exact rates. Plan for this upfront so it doesn't surprise you at the registrar's office.

Loan-to-value ratio

Lenders typically finance a percentage of the property's value (commonly up to 75–90% for eligible borrowers, varying by lender and loan amount), meaning you'll need funds for the remaining down payment plus the statutory registration charges.

Practical tips before applying

  • Get a rough loan pre-approval before you finalise a property, so you know your realistic budget.
  • Ask your lender specifically whether the plot or property you're considering is eligible for financing — DTCP-approved plots are generally easier to finance than unapproved ones.
  • Use an EMI calculator to check the monthly repayment against your income before committing.
  • Keep your registration-charge budget in a separate, readily accessible account — most lenders disburse the loan amount directly toward the property price, not the registration costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a home loan for an unapproved (non-DTCP) plot?

It's considerably harder. Most banks and housing finance companies are cautious about lending against layouts without DTCP or equivalent approval, since resale and legal risk is higher. Always confirm approval status before applying.

Does the home loan cover stamp duty and registration charges?

Generally no — these are typically paid by the buyer separately, out of pocket, in addition to the loan-financed property price. Budget for roughly 7.5% of the property value on top of your down payment.
